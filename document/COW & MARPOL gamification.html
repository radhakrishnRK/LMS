Module 1 · MARPOL Annex I Reg.33 · ISGOTT Ch.13
Purpose of COW — MARPOL, Commercial and Operational
Why COW exists, why it is mandatory, and why it benefits the vessel commercially
▶ READ
⚖️
MARPOL Regulation 33 — mandatory requirementCOW is mandatory under MARPOL Annex I Regulation 33 for crude oil tankers of 20,000 DWT and above built after 1982 (and many older vessels under Regulation 34 provisions). The vessel must have an approved COW Manual on board and conduct COW operations in accordance with that manual. Flag state can withdraw COW approval if procedures are not followed.
🛢️
What COW actually doesDuring discharge, crude oil cargo is used as the washing medium — pumped at high pressure through rotating nozzle machines fitted in the tank top. The crude oil jet dissolves and removes the heavy wax and sediment deposits (clingage) that stick to the tank walls and bottom during a voyage. This residue would otherwise remain as ROB and be lost to the vessel and charterer.
💰
Commercial advantage — reduced ROBWithout COW, a significant proportion of cargo (typically 0.3–1% of tank capacity) is left as clingage and sediment that cannot be pumped. COW dissolves this and recovers it during discharge, reducing ROB and improving the quantity delivered. Over a voyage of 200,000 MT, 0.5% ROB represents 1,000 MT of cargo — a significant commercial loss.
🌊
Environmental benefit — cleaner tanksCOW reduces the need for seawater tank washing at sea (which produces oily water that must go to slops). Cleaner tanks after COW mean less hydrocarbon contamination of the ballast water taken after discharge. This is why MARPOL mandates it — it is both more commercially effective and more environmentally sound than seawater washing.
📋
COW Manual — the governing documentEvery vessel required to conduct COW must have an approved COW Manual specific to that vessel. The Manual contains: system description, pre-COW procedures, washing sequences for each tank, monitoring requirements, safety procedures, emergency shutdowns, and record-keeping requirements. COW must be conducted exactly per the Manual.
🔒
COW limitationsCOW can only be conducted simultaneously with discharge — the drive fluid (cargo) must be available and the cargo pump must be running. COW cannot be conducted in ballast condition. The washings return to the cargo being discharged — there is no separate disposal of COW washings. This is why the operation is safe for MARPOL: no overboard discharge of washings.
MARPOL Reg.33(8) — the critical compliance point: The COW Manual must be approved by the flag state. Operating COW without the vessel's approved COW Manual on board is a deficiency that attracts port state control detention. The Manual is not a generic document — it is vessel-specific and must match the installed system exactly.

COW System Components
Know every component — what it does, where it is, and what happens when it fails

ComponentFunctionKey operating parameter
Fixed COW machines §13.2.1Permanently installed rotating nozzle machines in each tank, typically at the tank top, providing high-pressure crude oil jets to all tank surfaces. Most vessels have 2–4 machines per tank.Operating pressure: typically 8–12 bar. Rotation: 1 full cycle in 20–60 minutes depending on machine type. Nozzle angle covers full tank geometry.
Portable COW machinesTank entry portable machines used to supplement fixed machines for bottom washing or areas with poor fixed coverage. Used through existing tank fittings.Require confined space entry planning. Only used after main COW and atmosphere testing. Rarely needed if fixed coverage is adequate.
COW drive fluid supply lineDedicated piping system supplying crude oil (drive fluid) at pressure from the cargo pumps to the COW machines. Separate from the cargo discharge line.Pressure maintained by throttling the cargo pump while simultaneously discharging. Supply pressure must be maintained within COW Manual limits.
COW distribution valveControls which tanks receive drive fluid. Allows individual tank selection during the COW programme. Located at the tank top or COW line manifold.Open only valves for tanks being washed. Closed valves must be confirmed locked — driving fluid to an unwashed tank wastes drive pressure.
Stripping pumpSmall dedicated pump that removes the final low layer of cargo and washings from each tank after the main cargo pump can no longer maintain suction. Reduces ROB to minimum.Stripping is the final stage of each tank's discharge. Continue stripping until no more liquid comes to pump — typically 30–60 minutes per tank.
Crude oil meter / flow meterMeasures the volume of drive fluid used. Required for calculating COW drive fluid consumption and confirming adequate supply during the wash programme.Record in COW log. Total drive fluid used across the voyage is an ORB entry.
Vent / high velocity ventThe tank must be vented during COW to prevent overpressure as the washing machines operate inside the inerted tank. IGS maintains positive pressure — HV vents manage excess pressure relief.Tank must be inerted (O₂ <8%) throughout COW. IGS must be running. Never open tank hatches during COW.

Module 3 · COW Manual §3 · ISGOTT §13.3
Pre-COW Checks and Terminal Communication
Every item on this checklist is a mandatory gate — COW cannot begin until all are verified
▶ READ
📋
SSSCL COW section §13.3.1The Ship-Shore Safety Checklist must include a COW-specific section. Terminal must agree to COW before operations begin. Agreement includes: notification of which tanks will be COW'd, expected COW duration, and that the terminal is aware COW is in progress during discharge. Terminal has the right to refuse COW — if refused, this must be recorded.
💨
IGS confirmation §13.3.2IGS must be in operation and tank O₂ must be confirmed below 8% before COW begins in any tank. COW machines create a crude oil mist inside the tank — in the presence of oxygen above 8%, this mist is ignitable. IGS must remain operational throughout COW. Check O₂ of the specific tank before starting that tank's COW.
📐
COW manual confirmation §13.3.3The vessel's approved COW Manual must be on board and the specific tank washing programme must match the Manual. The duty officer must be familiar with the Manual procedures for the tanks being washed on this voyage. A COW operation not compliant with the Manual is a MARPOL violation.
⚙️
System readiness checks §13.3.4Before COW: confirm all COW machine drive fluid supply valves are in correct position; check COW line pressure is achievable; confirm stripping pump is on standby; confirm all overboard valves and non-COW tank isolation valves are closed; verify cargo pump can maintain both discharge and COW supply pressure simultaneously.
Never start COW without terminal agreement. MARPOL Regulation 33 requires that the terminal be informed before COW begins. If COW starts without notification and the terminal objects, the operation must stop immediately. Proceeding after a terminal refusal is a violation reportable to the flag state and Port State Control.

Module 4 · COW Manual §4 · ISGOTT §13.4
COW Sequence — Single and Multi-Stage Washing
The correct washing sequence determines whether COW achieves its purpose — getting it wrong leaves clingage behind
▶ READ
1️⃣
Top washing — first stage §13.4.1COW machines wash the upper portion of the tank while cargo level is high. The nozzles are set to cover the tank walls from the top down. Top washing must be completed while sufficient cargo remains to recirculate the washings. The COW Manual specifies the minimum cargo level required for top washing.
2️⃣
Bottom washing — second stage §13.4.2After the main cargo has been discharged to the bottom-wash level (typically 0.5–1.5m), machines are repositioned (where applicable) or continue operating to wash the tank bottom and lower strakes. The bottom wash removes the heavy sediment deposits that accumulate at the lowest points.
🔄
Multi-stage vs single-stage §13.4.3Most vessels conduct multi-stage COW — top wash during main discharge, then bottom wash as the tank empties. Single-stage COW washes throughout in one pass. The vessel's COW Manual specifies which method is approved for each tank based on machine placement and nozzle coverage.
⏱️
Washing duration §13.4.4The COW Manual specifies minimum washing durations for each tank. Typical durations: top wash 2–4 hours, bottom wash 1–2 hours. The duty officer must monitor and record start and stop times for each stage of each tank. Under-washing leaves clingage; over-washing wastes drive fluid with no additional benefit after machines have completed their rotation cycle.
The COW Manual washing programme is not optional. If a terminal requires faster discharge than the COW programme allows, the C/O must choose: either request slower discharge to allow COW, or abandon COW for that tank (and record this in the ORB). Partial COW that does not follow the approved programme must be documented and explained.

Module 5 · COW Manual §5 · ISGOTT §13.5
Monitoring, Simultaneous Discharge Safety and Emergency Shutdown
What the duty officer watches during COW — and the two situations that require immediate shutdown
▶ READ
ParameterNormal rangeAction levelRequired action
COW drive pressure §13.5.1Per COW Manual (typically 8–12 bar)Below minimum or above maximumBelow minimum: check pump performance, reduce discharge rate. Above maximum: immediate shutdown — overpressure can cause line failure.
Tank O₂ §13.5.2Below 8% throughoutAbove 8%Stop COW immediately. Check IGS status. Do not restart until O₂ confirmed below 8% and IGS fault investigated.
Tank deck pressure §13.5.3Positive (+100 to +500 mmWG)Negative or <+100 mmWGStop COW. Check IGS supply. Negative pressure can draw air into the tank through any opening — creates flammable atmosphere risk.
Drive fluid temperatureAmbient cargo temp — typically <60°CAbove maximum in COW ManualStop COW. Hot drive fluid can cause thermal expansion issues in the COW line. Allow to cool before restarting.
COW machine rotationConfirmed rotating per programmeNo rotation detectedStop that machine's drive supply. Check for blockage or mechanical failure. COW machine stuck in one position damages the tank coating by concentrated jetting.
⚡
Simultaneous COW and discharge — the pressure balanceRunning COW and discharging cargo from the same pump simultaneously requires careful pressure management. The cargo pump must maintain both discharge line pressure and COW supply pressure. If the terminal requests a higher discharge rate, COW pressure will drop. Inform the terminal immediately — do not silently allow COW pressure to fall below the Manual minimum.
🚨
Emergency shutdown of COW §13.5.5Stop COW immediately if: tank O₂ rises above 8%; COW line pressure exceeds maximum; any COW line or connection shows signs of leakage; the terminal calls ESD; general alarm sounds on the vessel; IGS pressure becomes negative. Closing the drive fluid supply valve to the COW machines is the immediate shutdown action.

Module 6 · MARPOL Annex I Reg.33 · ORB Part II · SIRE 2.0
Slop Management, MARPOL Compliance and COW Records
The ORB entries, SIRE questions, and COW Manual maintenance that determine compliance
▶ READ
📓
Oil Record Book Part II — COW entriesEvery COW operation must be recorded in ORB Part II under Code D (Crude Oil Washing). Required entries include: tanks washed, washing method (single/multi-stage), duration of each stage, COW machines used, and drive fluid quantity. ORB entries must be made at time of operation — not reconstructed. False or missing entries are a MARPOL criminal offence.
🏭
Slop management Reg.29COW washings return directly to the cargo being discharged — there are no separate washings to dispose of. However, tank bottom water and any residual washings from the last rinse go to the slop tank. Slops are processed through the Crude Oil Monitor (COM) before any overboard discharge — only if effluent is below 15 ppm per MARPOL Reg.34 conditions.
📋
COW completion recordAfter completing COW on each tank, a COW completion record must be signed by the duty officer. This records: tank identity, start/stop times for each wash stage, confirmation that the COW Manual programme was followed, any deviations and their reasons, and final ROB measurement after stripping. Retained with voyage documents.
📕
COW Manual maintenanceThe COW Manual must be kept up to date. Any changes to the COW system (machine replacement, line modification, nozzle angle change) must be reflected in the Manual and re-approved by the flag state. A COW Manual that does not match the installed system is non-compliant even if the Manual is itself approved. Annual review recommended.
SIRE 2.0 — Question (COW): "Is the vessel's COW Manual approved, on board, and followed for all COW operations?"
Inspector will check: Approved COW Manual on board (flag state approval stamp), Manual matches installed system (machine types, positions, and nozzle coverage correspond to actual system), ORB Part II COW entries (complete, contemporaneous, signed), COW completion records retained for inspection. Have the last three COW records ready for immediate review.
SIRE 2.0 — Question: "Does the officer responsible for COW understand the MARPOL requirements and the emergency shutdown procedure?"
Inspector will ask: What regulation mandates COW? (MARPOL Annex I Regulation 33). What are the two conditions that require immediate COW shutdown? (Tank O₂ above 8% and COW pressure outside Manual limits). What is the ORB Part II code for COW? (Code D — Crude Oil Washing). Must terminal be notified before COW? (Yes — SSSCL COW section must be agreed first).
