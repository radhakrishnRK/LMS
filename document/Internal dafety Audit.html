Purpose of Internal Safety Audits

Understand why ISM Code internal audits are conducted and how they support safe shipboard operations.

Training Visual
Core Learning
Audit is a safety toolAn internal audit checks whether the Safety Management System is implemented, understood and effective onboard. It is not a fault-finding exercise.
ISM Code expectationInternal audits support company and shipboard review, continual improvement, corrective action and verification that procedures are followed in practice.
Good audits prevent surprisesA strong internal audit finds weak signals before they become PSC, SIRE, class or incident findings.

Module 2 of 6 · Management / TMSA
Preparing for an Audit

Know how officers should prepare records, evidence, equipment areas and crew without staging or hiding reality.

Training Visual
Core Learning
Preparation is organisationAudit preparation means records are available, procedures are known, equipment areas are accessible and responsible persons can explain their work.
Evidence must be realCertificates, drills, permits, maintenance records, risk assessments, checklists and log entries must reflect actual practice.
Brief the crew honestlyCrew should understand the audit purpose and answer clearly from their own role. Coaching false answers creates bigger risk.

Module 4 of 6 · Management / TMSA
Common Findings and Root Causes

Recognise typical internal audit findings and the underlying causes behind repeated non-conformities.

Training Visual
Core Learning
Common findingsExpired certificates, incomplete drills, weak permit-to-work records, missing familiarisation, outdated risk assessments and poor corrective action tracking are frequent findings.
Root cause mattersThe real cause may be unclear responsibility, lack of training, poor supervision, inadequate planning or procedure complexity.
Observation versus non-conformityAn observation highlights improvement; a non-conformity identifies failure to meet a requirement. Both require proper follow-up.
Interactive Activity

Module 5 of 6 · Management / TMSA
Corrective Action and Follow-up

Learn how audit findings should be corrected, verified and closed without superficial compliance.

Training Visual
Core Learning
Correction is immediate fixCorrection removes the immediate problem, such as completing a missing update or replacing an expired notice.
Corrective action prevents recurrenceCorrective action addresses the reason the problem happened, such as training, responsibility, schedule control or procedure revision.
Close only after verificationA finding should be closed only when evidence shows the action was completed and is effective.

Module 6 of 6 · Management / TMSA
Audit Link to SIRE, PSC and TMSA

Understand how good internal audits improve external inspection readiness and management performance.

Training Visual
Core Learning
SIRE readinessSIRE 2.0 assesses what crew know, what they do and whether records match practice. Internal audits prepare the vessel by checking reality early.
PSC and class confidenceWhen records, procedures and crew understanding align, external inspectors see a controlled safety management system.
TMSA improvement loopTMSA expects measurement, analysis, improvement and management review. Audit findings feed this improvement cycle.
