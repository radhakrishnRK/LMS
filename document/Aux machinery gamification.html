Diesel Generators — Starting, Paralleling and Load Transfer

Understand starting air, lube oil pressure, cooling water, breaker closing, synchronising, load sharing and abnormal generator conditions.

READ
⚡
  • Pre-start checks include lube oil level and pressure, jacket-water availability, cooling water valves, fuel supply, starting air pressure and no active trips.
  • When paralleling, voltage, frequency and phase must be matched before closing the breaker. A wrong closure can damage alternator windings, coupling and switchboard equipment.
  • After connecting, load is transferred gradually. Watch kW, kVAR, frequency, voltage, exhaust temperatures and abnormal vibration.

Centrifugal Pumps — Priming, Cavitation and Seal Care

Recognise pump priming requirements, suction limitations, cavitation symptoms, discharge control and mechanical seal issues.

READ
💧
  • A centrifugal pump generally cannot pump air. The pump casing and suction line must be flooded or primed before starting unless it is a self-priming arrangement.
  • Cavitation occurs when suction pressure drops and vapour bubbles form and collapse inside the pump. Symptoms include rattling noise, fluctuating pressure, vibration and loss of performance.
  • Never throttle the suction valve to control flow. Control centrifugal pump output from the discharge side where applicable and as per manual.

Positive Displacement Pumps — Reciprocating and Gear Pumps

Know applications, relief valve importance, start-up precautions and hazards of blocked discharge in positive displacement pumps.

READ
🔩
  • Positive displacement pumps move a fixed quantity per stroke or revolution. They are used for viscous fluids, fuel oil transfer, lube oil, sludge and dosing.
  • Never start or run a positive displacement pump against a closed discharge unless a correctly set relief valve protects the system. Pressure can rise extremely quickly.
  • Relief valves protect the pump and line, but they are not operating controls. A lifting relief valve indicates a problem that must be investigated.

Air Compressors — Stages, Intercooling and Safety Devices

Understand main air compressor operation, two-stage compression, intercoolers, moisture separation, drains, relief valves and air receiver safety.

READ
🌬️
  • Marine starting-air compressors normally compress in stages with intercooling to reduce air temperature and improve efficiency.
  • Moisture and oil carry-over must be drained from intercoolers, separators and receivers. Water in starting air can cause corrosion and malfunction of air-start components.
  • Safety valves, high temperature trips, low lube oil pressure trips and automatic drains are critical safeguards. Never gag or bypass a safety device.

Fresh Water Generators, Purifiers and Heat Exchangers

Operate auxiliary systems with attention to salinity, conductivity, vacuum, interface control, back pressure, fouling and cleaning requirements.

READ
🧪
  • Fresh water generators depend on correct vacuum, heating medium, cooling sea water flow and salinity monitoring. High salinity or conductivity must divert production to bilge or dump, not to the fresh-water tank.
  • Purifiers require correct gravity disc or interface control, feed temperature, throughput and clean bowl condition. Water seal loss or wrong settings can cause oil loss to sludge tank.
  • Heat exchangers must be monitored for temperature approach, pressure drop, fouling and leakage. A sudden performance drop may indicate blocked tubes or poor flow.

Module 6 of 6 · STCW A-III/1 · Engine Department

Watchkeeping Rounds, Records, Reporting and PMS Awareness

Build disciplined auxiliary machinery rounds: observe, compare, record, trend and report before small abnormalities become failures.

READ
📋
  • A good round is not a walk-through. It checks sound, smell, vibration, temperature, pressure, leakage, level, filter differential pressure, alarms and standby readiness.
  • Record readings accurately and compare with previous trends. A slow increase in bearing temperature or pressure drop may be more important than one isolated value.
  • PMS awareness means knowing due tasks, running hours, critical spares, manual instructions and when to escalate defects or near misses.
