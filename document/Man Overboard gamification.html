🚨 Module 1: Initial Recognition & Immediate Actions

The first moments after a Man Overboard incident are critical. Immediate recognition and swift action significantly improve recovery outcomes.

Immediate Actions on Deck

  • SHOUT "MAN OVERBOARD" - Alert all crew immediately
  • POINT - Maintain visual contact and point continuously
  • THROW LIFE RING - Deploy nearest life ring toward person
  • MARK POSITION - Note vessel position and time
  • ALERT BRIDGE - Report to bridge immediately

Bridge Notification

The bridge must be notified immediately with clear, concise information:

  • Exact time of incident
  • Person's name and location
  • Vessel's position and heading
  • Sea state and weather conditions
  • Availability of rescue equipment
⚠️ Critical: Every second counts. Hypothermia begins immediately in cold water. Survival time decreases rapidly with water temperature.

🧭 Module 2: Vessel Manoeuvring & Bridge Procedures

Once MOB is reported to the bridge, immediate vessel manoeuvring is essential to return to the person and maintain visual contact.

Williamson Turn

The Williamson Turn is the most commonly used MOB manoeuvre for large vessels:

  • Hard rudder (full helm) in the direction of the person
  • When vessel has turned approximately 60°, reverse rudder to full opposite
  • Continue until vessel is on reciprocal course (180° from original heading)
  • Vessel approaches person from astern on original course
  • Advantage: Brings vessel back on original track

Anderson Turn

Used for smaller vessels or when speed needs to be reduced quickly:

  • Hard rudder in direction of person
  • When heading has changed 90°, reduce speed to minimum
  • Continue turn to bring vessel parallel to person
  • Approach from windward side

Scharnow Turn

Alternative for specific situations:

  • Hard rudder away from person initially
  • Turn 240° and approach from windward
  • Less commonly used but effective in certain sea states
Key Principle: The choice of manoeuvre depends on vessel type, sea state, wind direction, and visibility. The goal is to return to the person as quickly as possible while maintaining visual contact.

⛵ Module 3: Rescue Boat Operations & Recovery

Rescue boat launch and recovery operations require precise coordination and adherence to safety procedures.

Rescue Boat Launch Decision

  • Assess weather and sea state
  • Confirm person is conscious and responsive
  • Determine if person can stay afloat
  • Evaluate rescue boat crew readiness
  • Consider vessel's ability to manoeuvre

Rescue Boat Crew Duties

  • Coxswain: Commands boat, navigates to person, maintains communication
  • Bowman: Scans for person, deploys rescue equipment, assists recovery
  • Crew Members: Maintain visual contact, prepare recovery equipment, assist with person
  • Lookouts: Maintain continuous visual contact with person and boat

Approach and Recovery

  • Approach from windward when possible
  • Reduce speed to avoid creating wash
  • Position boat parallel to person at safe distance
  • Deploy recovery equipment (rescue net, ladder, or sling)
  • For unconscious person: Use rescue basket or stretcher
  • Avoid sudden movements that could injure person
⚠️ Hypothermia Consideration: Persons recovered from cold water may be unconscious or unresponsive. Handle gently to avoid cardiac complications. Provide immediate medical attention.

🌙 Module 4: Night MOB, Medical Response & Drills

Night MOB incidents present additional challenges. Medical response and regular drills are essential for readiness.

Night MOB Challenges

  • Reduced visibility of person in water
  • Difficulty maintaining visual contact
  • Increased risk of collision with rescue boat
  • Difficulty assessing person's condition
  • Increased stress on crew

Night MOB Search Patterns

  • Expanding Square Search: Systematic grid pattern expanding from last known position
  • Parallel Track Search: Vessel makes parallel passes through search area
  • Creeping Line Ahead: Vessel moves slowly forward while maintaining search pattern

Night MOB Lighting

  • Activate all deck lighting
  • Deploy searchlights to scan water
  • Use rescue boat spotlights
  • Activate person's life jacket lights
  • Consider flares or illumination rounds (if available)

Post-Recovery Medical Response

  • Hypothermia Treatment: Gradual rewarming, avoid rapid temperature changes
  • Exhaustion Management: Rest, hydration, monitoring
  • Unconscious Person: Recovery position, monitor airway, prepare for CPR
  • Medical Evacuation: Contact nearest medical facility, prepare for helicopter evacuation if needed

MOB Drill Requirements

  • Drills conducted at least quarterly
  • All crew members participate
  • Documented in ship's records
  • SIRE inspectors review drill records
  • Drills include all aspects: recognition, manoeuvring, rescue boat, recovery
✓ Key Learning: Regular drills ensure crew readiness and familiarity with procedures. Effective communication and coordination are essential for successful MOB response.
