🔥 Module 1: Boiler Types and Combustion Management

Water Tube Boilers

  • Water flows inside tubes, combustion gases outside
  • Higher efficiency and faster steam generation
  • Common on modern cargo vessels
  • Operating pressure: 15-35 bar
  • Burner control systems for combustion management

Composite Boilers

  • Combination of water tube and fire tube design
  • Improved heat transfer efficiency
  • Suitable for steam tankers
  • Operating pressure: 10-25 bar

Combustion Management

  • Burner control systems regulate fuel flow
  • Excess air monitoring prevents incomplete combustion
  • Flame monitoring ensures safe operation
  • Oxygen content: 2-3% in exhaust gases
  • Flame failure triggers automatic shutdown

💨 Module 2: Steam Generation and Distribution

Steam Generation Process

  • Water heated in boiler tubes to saturation point
  • Steam pressure controlled by safety valves
  • Superheated steam improves efficiency
  • Pressure range: 15-35 bar for cargo heating
  • Temperature: 200-250°C for saturated steam

Steam Distribution Systems

  • Main steam line carries steam from boiler
  • Branch lines supply cargo heaters and auxiliary equipment
  • Steam traps remove condensate from lines
  • Drain valves prevent water hammer
  • Pressure regulators maintain constant pressure

Pressure Control

  • Safety valves set at maximum operating pressure
  • Pressure gauges monitored continuously
  • Relief valves prevent overpressure
  • Automatic burner control maintains pressure

💧 Module 3: Water Treatment and Chemical Dosing

Boiler Water Treatment

  • Prevents scale formation and corrosion
  • Maintains water quality for efficient steam generation
  • Chemical treatment essential for long boiler life
  • Alkalinity maintained between 200-500 ppm
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) controlled below 3000 ppm

Chemical Dosing

  • Sodium phosphate prevents scale formation
  • Sodium hydroxide maintains alkalinity
  • Oxygen scavenger removes dissolved oxygen
  • Hydrazine or sodium sulfite used as oxygen scavengers
  • Dosing based on water test results

Blow-Down Procedure

  • Removes concentrated salts and impurities
  • Performed daily or as needed
  • Blow-down valve opened slowly to avoid thermal shock
  • Typically 5-10% of boiler water volume
  • Recorded in water treatment log

🧪 Module 4: Water Testing and Parameters

Water Testing Procedures

  • Alkalinity test measures pH and alkalinity
  • Chloride test detects salt contamination
  • Phosphate test verifies treatment chemical levels
  • Oxygen scavenger test confirms corrosion protection
  • Tests performed daily or per procedure

Key Parameters

  • Alkalinity: 200-500 ppm (as CaCO3)
  • Chloride: Less than 100 ppm
  • Phosphate: 10-30 ppm
  • Oxygen scavenger: 10-30 ppm
  • Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): Below 3000 ppm

Water Testing Equipment

  • Alkalinity test kit with indicator
  • Chloride test kit (silver nitrate method)
  • Phosphate test kit
  • Oxygen scavenger test kit
  • TDS meter for electronic measurement

⚠️ Module 5: Safety Devices and Emergency Procedures

Boiler Safety Devices

  • Safety valves set at maximum operating pressure
  • Low water alarm prevents dry firing
  • Flame failure detector stops fuel flow
  • Pressure relief valve prevents overpressure
  • Temperature sensors monitor boiler temperature

Emergency Shutdown Criteria

  • Pressure exceeds maximum operating limit
  • Water level drops below safe minimum
  • Flame failure detected
  • Abnormal vibration or noise
  • Fuel supply contamination detected

Boiler Shutdown and Lay-Up

  • Gradual cooling prevents thermal shock
  • Fuel supply isolated and purged
  • Boiler drained and inspected
  • Lay-up procedure preserves boiler internally
  • Soot blowing performed before shutdown
