📚 Module 1: Blackout Causes & Immediate Consequences

Duration: 10-12 minutes | XP: 50 points

Common Causes of Blackout

Overload: Excessive electrical load exceeds generator capacity, causing automatic shutdown.

Governor Failure: Loss of speed control leads to frequency instability and protective shutdown.

Fuel Fault: Fuel supply interruption, contamination, or pressure loss stops engine.

Human Error: Incorrect switchboard operation, maintenance mistakes, or improper load management.

Immediate Consequences of Blackout

Loss of Propulsion: Main engine loses power, vessel loses steerage and speed.

Loss of Steering: Hydraulic steering systems fail without electrical power.

Loss of Lighting: Navigation lights, deck lights, and interior lighting fail.

Loss of Navigation Aids: Radar, GPS, and electronic chart displays go dark.

⚙️ Module 2: Emergency Generator Operation

Duration: 12-14 minutes | XP: 50 points

SOLAS Automatic Start Requirements

Emergency generators must automatically start within 45 seconds of main power loss (SOLAS Chapter II-1, Regulation 42).

Automatic Starting System: Pressure switch detects low pressure, triggers starter motor.

Fuel Supply: Automatic fuel valve opens; fuel pressure and temperature monitored.

Load Shedding: Non-essential loads automatically disconnected to reduce startup load.

Manual Start Procedure

Step 1: Check fuel level and pressure.

Step 2: Verify cooling water circulation.

Step 3: Engage starter motor (manual push-button or key switch).

Step 4: Monitor engine parameters (RPM, temperature, pressure).

Step 5: Once running at rated speed, close fuel isolation valve on main engine.

Load Management

Essential Services Priority: Lighting, navigation aids, steering, communication.

Gradual Load Addition: Add loads incrementally to avoid overload.

Monitor Frequency: Maintain 50 Hz (or 60 Hz) ±2% during load pickup.

🔌 Module 3: Power Restoration Sequence

Duration: 14-16 minutes | XP: 50 points

Emergency Switchboard Essential Services

Emergency switchboard supplies critical loads when main power fails:

Priority 1 (Immediate): Navigation lights, steering, emergency lighting.

Priority 2 (Within 30 sec): Communication systems, radar, GPS.

Priority 3 (Within 2 min): Cargo heating, ventilation, auxiliary systems.

Main Switchboard Restoration Sequence

Step 1: Emergency generator running at rated speed and frequency.

Step 2: Verify emergency switchboard voltage and frequency stable.

Step 3: Check main engine fuel supply and cooling systems.

Step 4: Start main engine (if fuel fault cleared).

Step 5: Synchronize main generator with emergency generator.

Step 6: Transfer loads gradually from emergency to main switchboard.

Step 7: Stop emergency generator once main power fully restored.

Parallel Running & Load Sharing

Synchronization Check: Voltage, frequency, and phase angle must match within limits.

Load Sharing: Adjust governor and voltage regulator for equal load distribution.

Monitoring: Watch for circulating currents and unequal load distribution.

📡 Module 4: Bridge-ER Communication & Coordination

Duration: 10-12 minutes | XP: 50 points

Communication During Blackout

Immediate Bridge Notification: Chief Engineer reports blackout to Master immediately.

Status Updates: Provide regular updates on generator status and restoration progress.

Estimated Time to Restore: Inform bridge of expected restoration time.

Propulsion Status: Confirm when main engine can be restarted.

Bridge Actions During Blackout

Announce Blackout: Master announces "Blackout" on all communication systems.

Manual Steering: Prepare for manual steering if hydraulic power lost.

Reduce Speed: Reduce engine load to assist emergency generator startup.

Monitor Position: Use GPS backup or celestial navigation if available.

Engine Room Team Coordination

Chief Engineer: Directs emergency response and restoration sequence.

2nd Engineer: Operates emergency generator and monitors parameters.

ETO/3rd Engineer: Assists with switchboard operations and load management.

Clear Communication: Use standard terminology and confirm all instructions.

✅ Module 5: SIRE Compliance & Testing Records

Duration: 10-12 minutes | XP: 50 points

SOLAS Emergency Generator Testing Requirements

Weekly Test: Full load test for at least 2 hours under realistic conditions.

Load Transfer Test: Verify automatic load transfer from main to emergency generator.

Manual Start Test: Confirm manual start procedure works correctly.

Fuel Supply Test: Check fuel pressure and flow during operation.

Record-Keeping Requirements

SOLAS Log Entries: Record all generator tests, maintenance, and repairs.

Test Parameters: Document voltage, frequency, load, temperature, and pressure.

Defects & Repairs: Note any defects found and corrective actions taken.

Crew Participation: Record which crew members participated in testing.

SIRE Inspector Checks

Testing Records: Inspectors verify weekly testing records are complete and accurate.

Equipment Condition: Physical inspection of emergency generator and switchboard.

Crew Competency: Questions about blackout procedures and emergency response.

Compliance Evidence: Certificates of competency and training records reviewed.
