Module 1 of 6 · MARPOL Annex II · NLS operations

What Annex II Controls

Recognise when MARPOL Annex II applies and why NLS handling is different from oil cargo residues.

Visual briefing

ScopeMARPOL Annex II controls pollution from noxious liquid substances carried in bulk. On chemical and product tankers, the cargo category, P&A Manual and IBC Code entry drive the correct operation.
Crew pointDo not guess from the cargo name alone. Check the cargo document, IBC Code entry, ship's Certificates and P&A Manual before loading, tank cleaning or discharge.
Stop ruleIf the cargo category, special requirements or discharge instructions are unclear, stop and ask the responsible officer before opening valves or starting pumps.

NLS Categories X, Y, Z and OS

Understand the pollution meaning of Category X, Y, Z and Other Substances.

Visual briefing

Category XMajor hazard. Discharge into the marine environment is prohibited in normal residue operations and strict prewash/reception-facility handling normally applies at the unloading port.
Category YHazard to marine resources or human health, or harm to amenities/legitimate sea uses. Discharge is limited by strict conditions.
Category Z / OSCategory Z presents a minor hazard with less stringent restrictions. OS are evaluated as outside X, Y and Z, but shipboard procedures and records still matter.

P&A Manual and Cargo Record Book

Use the Procedures and Arrangements Manual and Cargo Record Book as operational control documents.

Visual briefing

P&A ManualThe Procedures and Arrangements Manual tells the ship how to unload, strip, prewash, tank clean and discharge residues for Annex II cargoes.
Cargo Record BookThe Cargo Record Book records loading, unloading, internal transfer, tank cleaning, prewash, discharge to reception facility, discharge to sea where permitted, and accidental releases.
Inspection mindsetA clean operation without correct entries can still become a deficiency. Documentation must match what actually happened.

Prewash, Stripping and Reception Facilities

Know when prewash is required and why efficient stripping matters.

Visual briefing

PrewashPrewash removes cargo residue before the vessel leaves the unloading port when required by category, cargo properties, port requirements or P&A Manual instructions.
StrippingModern stripping is intended to minimise residue left in tanks and associated piping. Poor stripping increases pollution risk and tank cleaning burden.
Reception facilityRequired residues and washings must be delivered ashore to a proper reception facility. Crew must not improvise a discharge route.

Discharge Criteria and Special Areas

Apply the basic no-discharge mindset and recognise when discharge is only allowed under strict conditions.

Visual briefing

Basic ruleDo not discharge NLS residues unless the responsible officer confirms that the cargo category, tank status, ship speed, distance from land, water depth, below-waterline outlet and P&A Manual conditions are all satisfied.
Antarctic areaAnnex II treats the Antarctic area as a special area with very strict controls. Crew must follow the voyage-specific environmental instructions.
Crew actionValve line-up mistakes are among the fastest ways to create pollution. Verify every line-up before starting a discharge or transfer.

Spill Prevention and Emergency Response

Control spills quickly and protect people, the ship and the environment.

Visual briefing

Immediate priorityRaise the alarm, stop the transfer if safe, close relevant valves, contain the spill, protect scuppers and inform the responsible officer.
Personal safetyNLS cargoes may be toxic, corrosive, flammable or reactive. Do not enter vapour clouds or attempt cleanup without PPE and instructions.
ReportingAccidental discharge or spill response must be reported and documented. Never hide or dilute a spill.
