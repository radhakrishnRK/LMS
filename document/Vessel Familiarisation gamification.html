Modules / Module 1

📋 Module 1: Why Familiarisation Matters

STCW Watchkeeping Obligation: You must not take over a watch until you are fully familiarised with the vessel, its systems, and emergency procedures.

SIRE 2.0 Inspector Questions: Inspectors ask every crew member about their familiarisation experience. This is a key compliance indicator.

ISM Code Requirement: Structured familiarisation is part of the vessel's Safety Management System (SMS).

Why It Matters: Proper familiarisation prevents accidents, ensures compliance, and builds crew confidence.

👥 Module 3: Rank-Specific Familiarisation Requirements

Master: Vessel operation, company procedures, regulatory compliance, crew management.

Deck Officers: Bridge systems, navigation equipment, cargo operations, safety procedures.

Engineers: Engine room systems, propulsion, power generation, maintenance procedures.

Ratings: Safety equipment, emergency procedures, work station locations, basic vessel systems.

Modules / Module 4

📝 Module 4: Handover Period & Sign-Off

Handover Period Duration: Typically 2-4 weeks depending on rank and vessel type.

What Must Be Covered: All checklist items must be completed and signed off before independent duties.

Signing the Checklist: Both the new crew member and the responsible officer must sign and date the familiarisation checklist.

Legal Requirement: STCW requires that watchkeeping personnel are not to take over a watch until familiarised.

Modules / Module 5

🔄 Module 5: MOC & SIRE Compliance

Management of Change (MOC): New crew joining is a change that requires managed familiarisation.

MOC Process: Identify the change (new crew), assess risks, implement controls (familiarisation), verify effectiveness.

SIRE Expectations: Inspectors expect to see documented familiarisation procedures and completion records.

Records: Keep signed and dated familiarisation checklists as evidence of compliance.
