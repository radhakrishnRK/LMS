Steering Gear Types & Hydraulic Systems

Ram Type Steering Gear

Ram type steering gears use a double-acting hydraulic ram connected to the rudder stock through a mechanical linkage. The pump unit provides pressurized hydraulic fluid to move the ram in both directions, creating rudder movement.

Rotary Vane Steering Gear

Rotary vane systems use a rotating vane inside a housing to convert hydraulic pressure into rotational motion. This type is more compact and provides smoother operation compared to ram systems.

Hydraulic System Components

The hydraulic system consists of pump units, actuating cylinders, control valves, hunting gear, and accumulators. Proper maintenance of these components is critical for safe steering operation.

SOLAS Testing Requirements

Weekly Full Movement Test

SOLAS regulations require a full movement test of the steering gear every week. This test verifies that the steering gear can move from hard-a-port to hard-a-starboard and back, confirming full operational capability.

Pre-Departure Test

Before departure, the steering gear must be tested to ensure proper operation. This includes checking rudder angle indicators and verifying communication between bridge and steering gear room.

Rudder Angle Indicator Check

The rudder angle indicator must be checked and calibrated to ensure accurate feedback to the bridge. Any discrepancies must be recorded and corrected before departure.

Emergency Steering Operations

Steering Gear Changeover

In case of main steering gear failure, the vessel must be able to switch to emergency steering. This involves transferring control from the bridge to the emergency steering position and activating the backup steering system.

Emergency Steering Position

The emergency steering position is typically located in the steering gear room. It must be clearly marked, easily accessible, and equipped with a rudder angle indicator and communication system.

Communication Procedures

Clear communication between the bridge and emergency steering position is critical. Standard procedures must be established and regularly drilled to ensure quick and effective response during emergencies.

Failure Modes & Troubleshooting

Hydraulic Leak

Hydraulic leaks reduce system pressure and steering response. Signs include slow rudder movement, unusual noises, and visible fluid leakage. Immediate inspection and repair are required.

Pump Failure

Pump failure results in loss of hydraulic pressure and complete steering loss. This is a critical emergency requiring immediate activation of emergency steering procedures.

Control System Failure

Control system failures affect steering response and may prevent proper rudder movement. These can include valve failures, electrical failures, or mechanical linkage problems.

Record-Keeping & Compliance

SOLAS Log Entries

SOLAS regulations require detailed logging of all steering gear tests and maintenance. These records must include the date, time, test results, and any defects found. Records must be retained for inspection by port state control.

SIRE Inspector Requirements

SIRE (Ship Inspection Report) inspectors will review steering gear testing records as part of their comprehensive vessel inspection. Proper documentation is essential for passing SIRE inspections.

Maintenance Records

All maintenance activities, repairs, and component replacements must be documented. These records demonstrate compliance with maintenance schedules and help track system reliability.
